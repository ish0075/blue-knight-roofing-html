The Simple Inspection Schedule That Saves Thousands

Your roof is your home's first line of defense against Ontario's harsh weather. Yet most homeowners only think about it when something goes wrong. By then, a small $300 repair has become a $10,000 problem.

The good news? A simple roof inspection schedule can extend your roof's lifespan by years and catch problems while they're still inexpensive to fix. Here's exactly when you should inspect your roof.

The Standard Inspection Schedule

For most Ontario homes, twice-yearly inspections provide the best balance of prevention and practicality.

Spring (April-May): Assess winter damage from ice, snow, and freeze-thaw cycles

Fall (September-October): Prepare for winter and check for summer storm damage

When to Inspect More Frequently

Certain situations call for more frequent inspections:

If your roof is over 15 years old, inspect it 3-4 times per year. Aging materials deteriorate faster and require closer monitoring.

Inspect after severe weather: heavy winds (80+ km/h), hailstorms, ice storms, or heavy snowfall (30+ cm).

Properties with overhanging trees need quarterly inspections for debris accumulation and branch damage.

If you've had leaks before, inspect every 3-4 months to catch recurring issues early.

What Professionals Check During Inspections

Understanding what a thorough inspection includes helps you appreciate its value:

Professional inspections include written reports, photographs, and specific recommendations for repairs or maintenance.

• Shingle condition (cracks, curling, missing pieces)

• Flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights

• Gutters and downspouts

DIY Inspection: What You Can Check Yourself

Between professional inspections, homeowners can spot obvious issues:

⚠️ Safety First: Never climb on your roof without proper safety equipment. Professional roofers have the training and gear to inspect safely. One slip can result in serious injury.

From the Ground: Use binoculars to check for missing or damaged shingles

Interior: Look for water stains on ceilings, especially after rain

Gutters: Check for excessive granule accumulation when cleaning
