Frequently Asked Questions About Roof Inspection & Maintenance

How often should I have my roof inspected?

We recommend professional roof inspections at least twice a year—in spring and fall. Additional inspections are advisable after severe storms or if you notice any signs of damage or leaks.

What does a roof inspection include?

Our comprehensive inspection covers: shingles/roofing material condition, flashing around vents, chimneys, and skylights, gutter and downspout condition, soffit and fascia, attic ventilation, signs of water damage or leaks, and structural integrity.

Do you offer pre-purchase roof inspections?

Yes, we provide detailed roof inspections for home buyers. Our report includes current condition, estimated remaining lifespan, any needed repairs, and approximate costs—essential information for your purchase decision.

What's included in a maintenance program?

Our annual maintenance programs typically include: two inspections per year, debris removal, gutter cleaning, minor repairs (caulking, nail pops, small shingle issues), moss/algae treatment if needed, and priority scheduling for any repairs.

Is the inspection free?

We offer free inspections when combined with repair work. Standalone inspections have a nominal fee that is credited toward any repairs you choose to have done.
