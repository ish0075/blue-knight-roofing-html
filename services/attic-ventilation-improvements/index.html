Attic & Ventilation Improvement Services

Poor attic ventilation is a hidden roof killer. Trapped heat and moisture shorten your roof's lifespan, cause ice dams in winter, and drive up energy costs year-round. We design and install balanced ventilation systems that protect your roof and lower your bills.

Key Features of Our Attic & Ventilation Improvements

  • Ridge Vent Installation: Continuous ridge ventilation for maximum airflow along the entire roof peak.
  • Soffit Vent Upgrades: Intake ventilation through soffit panels to create balanced airflow through the attic.
  • Insulation Assessment: Evaluate attic insulation levels and recommend upgrades for optimal performance.
  • Ventilation Audit: Complete assessment of intake and exhaust ventilation to identify imbalances.

Benefits of Professional Attic & Ventilation Improvements

  • Extend Roof Life: Proper ventilation adds years to your roof
  • Lower Energy Bills: Reduce summer cooling costs by 10-30%
  • Prevent Ice Dams: Balanced ventilation stops ice dam formation
  • Prevent Mold: Remove trapped moisture that causes mold

Frequently Asked Questions About Attic & Ventilation Improvements

How do I know if my attic ventilation is inadequate?

Signs include: extremely hot attic in summer (over 60°C), ice dams in winter, peeling exterior paint, mold in attic, buckled or curling shingles, and high energy bills. We offer free ventilation assessments.

What is balanced ventilation?

Balanced ventilation means equal intake (soffit vents) and exhaust (ridge or box vents). This creates natural airflow that removes heat and moisture. Imbalanced systems are ineffective or can worsen problems.

Can I mix different types of exhaust vents?

Generally no. Mixing ridge vents with powered fans or box vents can short-circuit airflow. We design systems using one exhaust type with proper intake for optimal performance.

Will better ventilation really lower my energy bills?

Yes. In summer, proper attic ventilation can reduce attic temperatures by 20-30°C, significantly reducing cooling costs. In winter, it prevents moisture buildup that compromises insulation effectiveness.
